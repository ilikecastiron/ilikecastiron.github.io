About Jack

He even has his own holiday. November 19th his Kilby STEM day in the science community, one that many high schools will celebrate. He earned his Bachelor of Science from the University of Illinois and earned his Master of Science in Electrical Engineering from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee. He was also a World War II Veteran. He worked as a Technician for the Army, and when he was finished in the military, he went on to work as an Electrical Engineer. He won the Nobel Prize in 2000 for his accomplishments and was one of the first ones in his field to be awarded such an honor. The integrated circuit was also being manifested in someone else’s mind around the same time that Kilby was working on his idea. The name of this person is Robert Noyce, who was in California. They had the same idea of reducing the number of components that go into computing, but Kilby was able to construct is idea into a real working piece first. Noyce did ultimately design a more probable and useable device, but without Kilby’s initial design, it could be possible that nobody was able to create tge same thing.

The story of his invention of the integrated circuit is quite comical. He was a newer employee at Texas Instruments, hardly a few months. It was during the common summer vacation time for the more tenured employees, leaving Jack to stay at work, since he didn’t have the option to leave. With the extra time to himself he was able to focus on his new idea. He was able to build a makeshift integrated circuit, and it is noted that without this extra time alone, he possibly wouldn’t have the time to come up with the idea at all. This invention is what lead to so many great things in our world. Cell phones, calculators, space travel and pretty much any electronic device to date. He was able to fix the problem at the time; too many wires and components to realistically build a better computer design. He was able to build the necessary parts onto a single piece and was the starting point at making electronics much smaller than ever before.

After his invention of the integrated circuit, Kilby also helped invent the portable calculator and the thermal printer. He had roughly 50 patents to his name. He was able to show off the use of his integrated circuit in the portable calculators that he was able to help create, ultimately being a huge breakthrough for Texas Instruments. He is not the most famous person on the list of our Computer/Computing forefathers, but his invention has to be one of the most significant, in terms of what has been accomplished since its creation. Kilby was also someone that you would not guess he would or has won the Nobel Prize, due to how humble of an individual he was. When he won the award, and gave his speech, he said that “If Robert Noyce were alive, he would be up here with me accepting this award”. To me that goes to show how modest and kind of a man he was. The two of them are often thought of as the co-inventers of the integrated circuit. Even with him winning the Nobel Prize, it took him 42 years to be awarded that honor.
