Jack St. Clair Kilby

JackKilby.com

Jack Kilby was an American Electrical Engineer famous for his invention of the integrated circuit in 1958. He was one of a few that worked to create what we know as today as the microchip. He invented the first working one, and without his work the computing world could be completely different. He helped invent the handheld calculator after to show the usefulness of the newly made circuit and helped invent the thermal printer as well. Kilby was born in Great Bend, Kansas in 1923 and was drawn to the interests of electronics from a storm that struck his town when he was young. His father worked for the local power company and used radio to communicate with people that lost power.
